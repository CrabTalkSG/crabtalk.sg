Why Manila Clams Are Popular

Manila Clams are popular because they have a naturally sweet flavour and tender bite. When cooked, they release a rich seafood broth that improves soups, sauces and noodle dishes. This makes them useful even when they are not the main dish.

They are also easy to share. A kilogram of clams can be served as a side dish for several people, added to bee hoon, cooked in clear soup, or used as part of a larger seafood spread with crabs, lobsters, oysters and fish.

Best Ways to Cook Manila Clams

Chinese Wine Clams

Chinese wine clams are fragrant, comforting and one of the most popular ways to enjoy fresh clams. The wine brings out the sweetness of the seafood broth.

Garlic Butter Clams

Garlic butter is rich and aromatic. It works well when paired with bread, noodles or pasta because the sauce absorbs the natural clam juices.

Clear Soup Manila Clams

A clear soup preparation is simple but effective. The clam broth gives the soup sweetness without needing heavy seasoning.

XO Sauce Stir-Fry

For stronger flavour, XO sauce gives the clams a spicy, savoury kick that works well for sharing dishes.

How to Choose Good Manila Clams

Fresh clams should look moist, clean and alive. The shells should generally be closed or close when handled. Avoid clams with broken shells or strong unpleasant odours. Because clams are shellfish, freshness matters greatly.

How Much Manila Clams Should You Buy?

For a light side dish, 1kg can usually serve 2 to 4 people depending on the meal. If clams are the main shellfish dish, or if you are cooking for a larger group, you may want more. If the clams are being used in soup or noodles, the same quantity can stretch further because the broth becomes part of the dish.

For seafood gatherings, Manila Clams pair well with live mud crabs, yellow roe crabs, Boston lobsters, Soon Hock fish and oysters. They add variety and are easy for guests to share.

Tip: If you are planning to cook clams at home, rinse and purge them properly before cooking to reduce sand and improve the final dish.
