When buying live mud crab, customers usually look at activity level, shell condition, size, weight and intended cooking style. A good crab should be lively where possible, suitable in size for the number of diners and appropriate for the dish you want to cook.

Best Cooking Styles for Live Mud Crab

Live mud crab is one of the most versatile seafood choices in Singapore. It works well with rich sauces, wok-fried local flavours and simple steaming.

For customers who want to enjoy the crab’s natural sweetness, steaming is a clean option. For a stronger local seafood experience, chilli crab, black pepper crab and ginger onion crab are popular choices.
