Why Soon Hock Is Considered a Premium Fish

Soon Hock is prized for its firm but delicate flesh, natural sweetness and excellent steaming quality. In Chinese seafood dining, it is often associated with restaurant-style meals, family celebrations and premium banquet dishes.

The fish has a distinctive texture that holds well during steaming. When cooked properly, the flesh separates cleanly and remains moist. This is why many customers prefer live Soon Hock instead of frozen or pre-packed fish when planning an important meal.

Best Ways to Cook Soon Hock

Hong Kong-Style Steamed Soon Hock

This is one of the most popular cooking styles. Light soy sauce, hot oil, spring onion and ginger bring out the natural sweetness of the fish without covering its texture.

Teochew-Style Steamed Soon Hock

Teochew-style steaming usually includes ingredients such as preserved vegetables, tomatoes and sour plum. It gives the dish a savoury and slightly tangy profile.

Ginger Scallion Soon Hock

Ginger and spring onion are classic pairings for premium fish. This style is aromatic but still clean enough to let the fish shine.

Fish Soup or Broth

For customers who enjoy lighter meals, Soon Hock can also be used in premium fish soup where the broth carries the sweetness of the fish.
