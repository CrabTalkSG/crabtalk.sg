How Alaska King Crab Differs From Mud Crab

Many customers compare Alaska King Crab with Sri Lanka Yellow Roe Crab or local-style mud crabs. They are very different eating experiences. Mud crabs are prized for the body meat, claws and roe, and they are excellent for chilli crab, black pepper crab, steamed crab and ginger onion crab. Alaska King Crab is usually enjoyed for its long legs, thick meat fibres and clean sweetness.

If you want a strong local crab dish with sauce, mud crab is usually the better choice. If you want a premium sharing seafood item where everyone can enjoy large pieces of sweet leg meat, Alaska King Crab is the more luxurious option.

Best Cooking Styles for Alaska King Crab

Simple Steaming

Steaming is the best way to enjoy the natural sweetness of Alaska King Crab. It keeps the meat moist and lets the premium flavour stand on its own.

Garlic Butter

Garlic butter is rich, fragrant and popular for customers who prefer a stronger Western-style seafood flavour.

Grilled King Crab Legs

Grilling gives the shell a light roasted aroma while keeping the crab meat firm and sweet. It works especially well for leg sections.

Seafood Platter

For parties, Alaska King Crab can be served with oysters, lobsters, Manila clams and prawns to create a premium seafood spread.
