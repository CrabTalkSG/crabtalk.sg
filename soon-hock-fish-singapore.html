Complete Soon Hock Fish Buying Guide for Singapore

Soon Hock, also known as Marble Goby, is one of the most prized fish in Chinese seafood dining. It is loved for its firm flesh, natural sweetness and excellent texture when steamed. In Singapore, Soon Hock is often chosen for family meals, birthdays, celebrations and restaurant-style home cooking because it feels premium without needing complicated preparation.

At Crab Talk, live Soon Hock is available from $68 per kg onwards, with fish usually from about 1kg and above. Availability changes daily, so customers should WhatsApp us before visiting if they need a specific size or collection timing.

Why Soon Hock Is Considered Premium

Soon Hock has a distinctive texture that separates it from many common market fish. The flesh is firm yet delicate, with a naturally sweet flavour that suits steaming. When handled correctly, it has a clean taste and absorbs light sauces very well. This is why many Chinese restaurants use Soon Hock for premium steamed fish dishes.

  • Firm and sweet flesh
  • Excellent for steaming
  • Popular for Chinese banquet-style dishes
  • Suitable for home cooking and special meals
  • Best enjoyed live and freshly prepared

Best Cooking Styles for Soon Hock

Hong Kong-Style Steamed Soon Hock

This is the most popular preparation. Light soy sauce, hot oil, ginger and spring onion highlight the natural sweetness of the fish.

Teochew-Style Steamed Soon Hock

Teochew steaming uses ingredients such as salted vegetables, tomato, sour plum and ginger to create a savoury and lightly tangy flavour.

Ginger Scallion Soon Hock

This style is fragrant, comforting and suitable for customers who prefer stronger aromatics while still keeping the fish flavour clear.

Fish Soup

Soon Hock can also be used for premium fish soup, especially when customers want a naturally sweet broth and firm fish slices.

Why Buy Soon Hock Live Instead of Pre-Cut Fish?

For premium fish such as Soon Hock, buying live gives customers the best control over freshness and texture. Steamed fish is a simple dish, so the quality of the fish matters more than heavy seasoning. When the fish is fresh, the flesh holds together better, tastes sweeter and gives a cleaner finish. This is why many customers prefer live Soon Hock for important meals rather than pre-cut or frozen options.

At Crab Talk, customers can view the fish in the tank before purchase. This makes it easier to choose based on size, budget and cooking plan. If you are cooking for elderly family members, children or guests, our team can help recommend a practical size and cooking style.

What To Serve With Soon Hock

Soon Hock works well as the main fish dish in a seafood meal. For a balanced spread, customers often pair it with Manila clams for soup or steaming, live mud crabs for a richer crab dish, and lobsters for noodles or garlic butter cooking. This gives the meal a mixture of clean steamed fish, sweet shellfish and stronger seafood flavours.
