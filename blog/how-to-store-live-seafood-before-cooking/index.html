How to Store Live Seafood Before Cooking

A practical guide to storing live seafood before cooking, including crabs, lobsters, clams and fish, with safe handling tips for Singapore home cooks.

5 min readUpdated May 2026Crab Talk Guide

Quick Takeaways

✓Cook live seafood as soon as possible for best quality.
✓Do not soak live crabs or lobsters in tap water.
✓Keep seafood cool, ventilated and separate from ready-to-eat food.

General rule: cook as soon as possible

Live seafood is best cooked as soon as possible after purchase. The longer it waits, the more quality can drop. If you are buying premium crab or lobster, plan your cooking time before collecting the seafood.

How to hold live crabs temporarily

Keep live crabs cool and shaded. Do not place them in freshwater or sealed plastic bags for long periods. They need air and stable conditions. If the crab is tied, keep it secure until cooking to reduce stress and injury.

How to hold live lobsters temporarily

Lobsters should be kept cool and damp, not submerged in tap water. Avoid sudden temperature changes. If you are not cooking immediately, ask Crab Talk for the best short-term holding method based on the lobster type.

How to store clams

Clams should be kept cool and allowed to breathe. Do not seal them tightly in an airtight container. Rinse only when preparing to cook, and discard any clams that smell bad or do not respond normally.

Food safety at home

Keep raw seafood away from cooked food, use clean utensils and wash hands after handling. If seafood smells sour, rotten or unusually strong, do not cook it. When in doubt, ask before buying and cook promptly.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I keep live crab overnight?

It is better to cook live crab as soon as possible. Overnight holding can reduce quality and increase mortality risk.

Can I put live seafood in tap water?

No. Tap water can stress or kill marine seafood such as crabs and lobsters.

What is the safest storage advice?

Keep seafood cool, ventilated and cook it promptly.
