What is Soon Hock fish?

Soon Hock is a premium fish that many Singapore families order for celebrations, home dinners and steamed fish dishes. It is appreciated for its firm flesh, clean taste and natural sweetness when steamed properly.

Unlike stronger-tasting fish, Soon Hock is usually enjoyed with lighter seasoning. A simple Hong Kong-style steam with soy sauce, ginger and spring onion is enough when the fish is fresh.

Why is Soon Hock fish expensive?

Soon Hock can be more expensive than common table fish because good-sized live fish are not always easy to secure. Supply changes by shipment, size, season and daily demand.

Customers are also paying for texture. Soon Hock has firm, refined flesh that works well for steamed fish, where freshness and handling are easy to taste. If the fish is live and in good condition, it naturally commands a higher price.

Best way to cook Soon Hock

The safest and most popular cooking style is Hong Kong-style steaming. Light soy sauce, ginger, spring onion and hot oil keep the dish clean and let the fish flavour come through.

For premium fish like Soon Hock, avoid overpowering the fish with heavy sauces unless that is your personal preference. The main reason people choose Soon Hock is the texture and clean sweetness.
