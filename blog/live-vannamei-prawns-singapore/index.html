What Are Vannamei Prawns?

Vannamei prawns are widely known as Pacific white shrimp or whiteleg shrimp. They are popular because the flesh is naturally sweet, firm and versatile enough for steaming, boiling, stir-frying, grilling, hotpot and noodle dishes.

Name

Vannamei / Whiteleg Prawn

Different sellers may use “prawn” or “shrimp,” but they are referring to the same commonly farmed species.

Texture

Firm and Bouncy

Careful cooking helps retain the firm bite that prawn lovers look for.

Flavour

Clean Natural Sweetness

Simple cooking methods allow the prawn flavour to remain the focus.

~20prawns per kg

How to Read Prawn Size by Count

When a seller states an approximate number of prawns per kilogram, a lower count normally indicates larger individual prawns. A batch at about 20 pieces per kg is visually substantial and suitable for a premium home meal. Because live seafood is not manufactured to identical dimensions, the final count may vary slightly.

Live vs Chilled vs Frozen Prawns

All three can be useful, but they suit different priorities. The main advantage of a live batch is that customers can observe the seafood condition before purchase. Chilled prawns are convenient for near-term cooking, while frozen prawns are practical for longer storage.

Live

Best for immediate premium cooking

Supply is limited and reservation is usually necessary.

Chilled

Convenient for short-term use

Quality depends on handling, temperature and time since harvest.

Frozen

Best for freezer convenience

Useful when consistent availability matters more than a live buying experience.

Five Good Ways to Cook Large Live Prawns

Salt-water boil with minimal seasoning
Steam with garlic and spring onion
Har lok with savoury-sweet sauce
Hotpot or steamboat
San Lou Hor Fun with wok hei
Light grill with butter or olive oil

The key is timing. Large prawns become dry and tough when overcooked.
