Why 31 Commonwealth Crescent Market is worth visiting

31 Commonwealth Crescent Market & Food Centre has the feel of an old-school Singapore neighbourhood food centre. It is not a polished shopping-mall food court; it is more practical, local and direct. Visitors come for daily meals, roast meats, noodles, chicken rice, kway chap and seafood options around the market.

For Crab Talk customers, the location is especially useful because you can choose live seafood from tanks, ask about today’s sizes and stock, then decide whether to bring the seafood home or arrange cooking nearby when available.

Stall operating hours can change without notice. For popular stalls, go earlier and check directly before making a special trip.

Popular stalls to try at or around 31 Commonwealth Crescent

Besides Crab Talk and Fai Kee, these stalls help make the market worth visiting. The notes below are based on current public food coverage and local dining mentions available online, but customers should still check each stall’s latest hours before travelling.

Roast meat

Foong Kee Traditional Charcoal Roaster

Foong Kee is a well-known charcoal roast meat name now associated with Commonwealth Crescent. It is known for Cantonese-style roast meats such as char siew, roast pork and roast duck.

Common review sentiment: people look for smoky roast-meat flavour, caramelised char siew and old-school Cantonese roast quality.
Chicken rice

Seng Kee Chicken Rice 新奇鸡饭

Seng Kee Chicken Rice is listed at Commonwealth Crescent Market & Food Centre and is a practical choice for customers who want a simple rice or noodle meal before or after buying seafood.

Common review sentiment: diners mention affordable chicken rice, curry chicken noodle style dishes and a neighbourhood hawker feel.
Noodles

SK Noodle House

SK Noodle House is another useful stall to include in a Commonwealth Crescent food trail, especially for visitors looking for old-school noodles in the morning or lunch period.

Common review sentiment: diners mention prawn noodles, fishball noodles and a traditional hawker noodle style.
Kway chap

Kian Meng Kway Chap 健民粿汁

Kian Meng Kway Chap is listed at 31 Commonwealth Crescent and is useful for visitors who enjoy kway chap, braised items and pig organ soup.

Common review sentiment: diners mention hearty portions, braised flavours and classic hawker-style kway chap.
