Best Seafood for Home Cooking in Singapore

Quick Takeaways

✓Choose seafood based on cooking method, not only price.
✓Clams and prawns are easier for quick home meals; crabs and lobsters suit special occasions.
✓Ask Crab Talk for cleaning, cutting and cooking-style advice before buying.

Easy seafood for beginners

For simple home cooking, Manila clams, prawns and fish are usually easier than large crabs or lobsters. They cook faster, require less handling and work well with garlic, ginger, black bean, chilli or steaming styles.

Best seafood for special occasions

Live crabs, Yellow Roe Crabs and lobsters are better for special meals where presentation and freshness matter. They create a stronger dining experience and are more memorable for family gatherings, celebrations or weekend meals.

Crab bee hoon for home-style sharing

Crab bee hoon is a strong choice when you want a comforting sharing dish. The noodles absorb crab sweetness and the dish feels generous without needing many separate items. The key is choosing a crab with good meat and shell freshness.

Fish and clams for lighter meals

Soon Hock, grouper and clams are good when you want cleaner flavours. Steaming fish keeps the meal light, while clams can be quickly cooked with garlic, chilli, ginger or Chinese wine-style aromatics.

How to plan before visiting

Before visiting Crab Talk, decide how many people are eating, whether you are cooking at home, and whether you want light, rich, spicy or premium flavours. This helps the stall recommend the right seafood from the day’s live stock.
