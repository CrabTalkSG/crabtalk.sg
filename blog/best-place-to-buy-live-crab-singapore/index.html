Why Buying Live Crab Matters

Freshness makes a major difference when it comes to crab. Live crabs generally offer better meat texture, natural sweetness and eating quality compared with frozen or poorly handled seafood. Once a crab dies, freshness drops quickly and the texture can become weak, watery or less satisfying.

When customers visit Crab Talk, they can compare live crabs in the tanks, ask about size and weight, and choose based on the intended cooking style. This is especially useful for family meals, celebrations, weekend dinners or special occasions.

How To Choose Good Live Crab

Choosing live crab is not only about size. A large crab is not automatically the best crab. Experienced seafood sellers look at weight, shell firmness, activity level and overall condition. Good crabs should feel dense for their size, have healthy shell condition and show signs of proper handling.

Customers often ask whether they should choose roe crab or meat crab. The answer depends on what they want to eat. If you love creamy roe and richer flavour, a premium female roe crab may be the better choice. If you want large claws and more meat for sauce-based dishes, a meat crab may be more suitable.

Yellow Roe Crab vs Meat Crab

Sri Lanka Yellow Roe Crabs are popular among roe lovers because of their rich golden roe and creamy texture. These crabs are highly sought after when the roe condition is good. They are especially suitable for lighter preparations such as cold crab or grilled crab, where the roe remains the highlight.

Meat crabs are usually chosen for stronger sauce-based cooking styles. They are good for chilli crab, black pepper crab, butter crab and crab bee hoon because the meat and sauce work together. For customers who prefer claw meat and body meat over roe, meat crabs are often the better choice.

Popular Cooking Styles

Singapore customers enjoy crab in many styles. The most famous is chilli crab, where a thick savoury-sweet sauce is paired with mantou. Black pepper crab is another classic for customers who prefer stronger pepper flavours. Ginger and onion crab is a traditional zi char-style option that keeps the crab flavour relatively clean.

For premium roe crabs, many customers prefer simpler cooking. At Crab Talk, Yellow Roe Crab is best enjoyed as cold crab or grilled crab because these methods allow the roe to remain the main attraction. Too much sauce can cover the natural richness of the roe.

Why Prices Change Daily

Live crab prices change because supply is affected by weather, import cost, seasonality, demand and quality. Premium roe crabs can be more expensive when the supply of good roe condition is limited. Larger crabs with better density may also cost more because eating quality is higher.

For customers, the best approach is not always to chase the lowest price. It is better to ask what is good on that day, what sizes are available, and which crab suits the cooking style.
